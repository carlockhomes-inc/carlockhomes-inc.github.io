# Origin Story: The Roots of M-Lab & Words Atelier

## The Spark: "Words" and "Kawaraban" (Newsletters)

> "Even when I couldn't love the old-fashioned name of the workplace, I couldn't bring myself to hate the work itself."

### The Context
- **Location:** MAX Plaza (Air Conditioning Showroom)
- **Role:** Speaking/Proclaiming work (Words)
- **Key Activity:** Creating "Kawaraban" (Newsletters/Flyers)

### The Insight
The true joy came from **working with words** and **delivering information** through a personal medium like the *Kawaraban*. This format—personal, informative, and word-centric—is the spiritual predecessor to the "Words Atelier" concept.

### The Format: 1999-2000 Era (The "Analog-Digital" Bridge)
- **Timing:** Around 1999-2000 (The turn of the millennium).
- **Context:** Internal communication for MAX Plaza (Showroom).
- **Content:** New product announcements, exhibition notices.
- **Method:** A nostalgic mix of **handwriting** and early **Word/Excel** designs.
- **Tone:** "Maseteta" (Precocious). Mixing showroom news with deep life philosophies.
- **Key Message:** "The past can be changed" (過去はかえられる) — a precocious but powerful insight for a young creator.
- **Daily Ritual:** Reading **Konosuke Matsushita’s** words from a notebook every day. This spiritual backbone informed the content.

### Connection to M-Lab
This experience reveals a core DNA of the M-Lab brand:
1.  **Love for Language:** The power of words to connect and inform.
2.  **Philosophy x Information:** It wasn't just product news; it was a vehicle for *thought*.
3.  **Personal Broadcasting:** The *Kawaraban* model as a prototype for modern newsletters or blogs.
4.  **Human Touch:** Even in a technical environment, the *soft* power of words created meaning.
54.  **Joy of Learning Tools:** Just as you learned Word/Excel then, you are mastering AI now to create your "Modern Kawaraban."

### The Name: "Fumiko-Max"
- **Origin:** The name **"Fumiko-Max"** is a direct spiritual successor to **"MAX Plaza"**.
- **Significance:** It signifies that "MAX Plaza" was not just a workplace, but the *birthplace* of your creative identity.
- **Legacy:** Carrying the name "Max" means carrying the spirit of that era—where you learned to blend technology (AC/Word) with human warmth (Kawaraban/Words).

### The Mission: Writing for the Gentle
> "The gentler you are, the less you can say it out loud. That is why you need the means to *write*."
> (相手がいることだから、大きな声では言えない。優しいほど声に出せないから書くって手段をもってほしい)

- **The Problem:** Kind people often swallow their true feelings because they worry about hurting others or causing trouble.
- **The Paradox:** "But if I write it down, it leaves evidence. That can be scary too."
- **M-Lab's Answer:** The "Laboratory" is where you take raw, potentially hurtful feelings and **sublimate (昇華)** them into art, philosophy, or stories.
    -   **Raw Emotion:** "Evidence" of anger/pain (Private).
    -   **Sublimated Work:** "Medicine" for others (Public).
- **M-Lab's Role:** To provide the safe space (Secret Base) to process these feelings, and the tools (Words/AI) to transform them into something beautiful that helps others without hurting anyone.

### The Safeguard: "Digital Tattoo" Awareness
> "In the modern age, a 'Digital Tattoo' lasts forever. That is why we need a *Laboratory*."

The fear of leaving permanent, negative evidence ("Digital Tattoo") is valid. M-Lab mitigates this:
1.  **The Cooling Chamber:** Write raw emotions in the **Private (Local)** Second Brain first. It never leaves your PC until *you* choose.
2.  **The Filter (Fumiko-Max):** Use the persona as a protective suit. "Fumiko-Max" speaks universal truths, not personal attacks.
3.  **The Edit:** Unlike spoken words which cannot be taken back, written words in the Atelier can be polished until they are safe and helpful.
