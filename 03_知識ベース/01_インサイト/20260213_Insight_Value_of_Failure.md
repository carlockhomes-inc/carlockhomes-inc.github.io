# Insight: The Treasure in the Ruins (The Value of Failure)

## Why Record Failures?
> "Success makes you confident, but failure makes you wise."

The user intuitively understands that "records of failure might be more useful than good memories." In the context of a Second Brain and M-Lab, these are not scars to be hidden, but **assets**.

### 1. The "Anti-Pattern" (反面教師)
In engineering, knowing "what NOT to do" is often more valuable than knowing one way to do it right.
-   **Example:** "Don't click this button without saving" is a life-saving data point.
-   **Application:** A "Troubleshooting" or "Lessons Learned" section in the Second Brain.

### 2. The "Shadow" as a Teacher
The user mentioned "hurting others" (人を傷つけたかもしれない出来事). This suggests deep reflection.
-   **Growth:** Acknowledging past insensitivity or mistakes is the surest sign of maturity.
-   **Content:** These stories resonate deeply with others because everyone has regrets. Sharing them (vulnerably but safely) creates powerful connection.

### 3. The Power of "Choice" (Reframing Misfortune)
The user realized that past difficulties (e.g., relationships) were not just "bad luck" but **choices**.
-   **Insight:** "It wasn't that I wasn't blessed; it was a choice." (あまりめぐまれなかったというより、選択ですよね)
-   **Philosophy:** This shifts the narrative from "victim" to "owner." Owning the choice means owning the power to choose differently now.
-   **M-Lab Spirit:** This aligns perfectly with "The past can be changed" — by changing the *meaning* of the past from "misfortune" to "choice."

### 4. Resilience Evidence
Surviving a failure is proof of resilience.
-   **Record:** "I failed at X, but I am still here, and now I know Y."
-   **Value:** When facing a new challenge, reading about past failures helps reduce fear. "I survived that, I can survive this."

## How to Document "Failure" (Safe Framework)

Don't just write "I am bad." Use the **KPT** or **Post-Mortem** format:

| Section | Content |
| :--- | :--- |
| **The Event** | What happened? (Objective facts) |
| **The Feeling** | How did it feel? (Subjective emotion - vent here!) |
| **The Lesson** | What did I learn? (The "Gold") |
| **The Change** | What will I do differently? (The Action) |
